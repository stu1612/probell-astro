# Probell Nutrition — Brand & Experience

**Last updated: 14 May 2026**

---

## Purpose

Probell Nutrition is an American-directed supplement brand built around strength,
performance, and blue-collar identity. The kettlebell-shaped protein container is
the product. The brand is the person who picks it up.

The website is a **brand presentation and business development tool**. It is not
an ecommerce store. Its job is to generate genuine interest from gyms,
distributors, and retailers — and to communicate the brand identity with
complete confidence.

It is not a marketplace. It is not a content blog. It is not an animation
showcase. It is a **focused brand statement with a single conversion goal:
get the right people to make contact.**

---

## Brand Principles

### 1. Earned, not aspirational
This brand is for people who already show up. The site does not sell the idea
of fitness — it acknowledges the person who is already doing it. Every word
and image should feel like recognition, not motivation.

### 2. Product as identity
The kettlebell-shaped container is not a gimmick. It is the brand. It sits in
a gym, looks like equipment, and belongs there. The product is always present
— never hidden, never secondary.

### 3. Americana without parody
The flag wrap, the worn textures, the blue-collar tone — these are authentic,
not decorative. They signal a specific cultural identity. Do not exaggerate or
caricature this direction. It should feel genuine.

### 4. Confidence over hype
No countdown timers. No "LIMITED TIME OFFER." No aggressive fitness clichés.
The brand tone is disciplined, direct, and certain. It does not need to shout.

### 5. Image-led, text-supported
Photography does the emotional work. Copy confirms it. Never the other way
around. If the image needs a paragraph to explain it, the image is wrong.

## Photography Rules

These rules apply to every image on the site without exception.

### Colour grade
All photography must share the same cinematic grade:
- Deep blacks, crushed shadows
- Warm amber tone on highlights — skin, equipment, floor
- High contrast — no flat or evenly exposed images
- Desaturated but not black and white
- No bright whites anywhere in frame
- No clean studio lighting — hard directional sources only

### Environment
Lifestyle photography must be set in hardcore gym environments:
- Raw concrete or brick walls
- Worn rubber or concrete floors with chalk dust
- Iron weight plates, power racks, barbells
- Industrial overhead lighting
- No modern, clean, or commercial gym equipment
- No soft boxes, no lifestyle wellness aesthetic

### Athletes
- Mid-effort or post-effort — not posing
- Authentic physique — muscular, tattooed, real
- No smiling, no direct camera address
- Sweat, focus, exhaustion — real moments only

### Product
- Always the Americana flag wrap design
- Always the kettlebell-shaped container
- Never isolated on white
- Always in context — on a gym floor, in an athlete's hand

### What to never use
- Stock photography aesthetic
- Soft lighting or pastel tones
- Clean modern gym environments
- Wellness or lifestyle supplement photography
- Images that could belong to any other brand

## Experience Principles

### 1. Immediate recognition
The visitor must know what Probell is within three seconds. Brand, product,
identity — all communicated before any scrolling.

### 2. Sections tell a story in sequence
Each section builds on the previous one. The visitor moves from feeling the
brand → seeing the product → understanding the purpose → making contact.
Nothing is repeated. Nothing is out of order.

### 3. Border breakers create energy
Section boundaries are design moments, not dividers. Torn ink edges, product
image breakouts, diagonal cuts, and hard contrasts replace standard horizontal
divides. Every section transition is a deliberate decision.

### 4. Density over whitespace
This is not a minimal site. Sections are tight, content is dense, and negative
space is used for contrast — not as a default. The site should feel full without
feeling cluttered.

### 5. Photography leads, UI follows
The site design exists to present photography. Navigation, typography, and
components are the frame — the image is always the subject.

## Target Audience — Primary

**Gyms, distributors, and retailers** interested in stocking Probell Nutrition.

The site is a business development tool first. Consumer appeal is secondary —
but consumer appeal is what makes the B2B case. A gym owner needs to believe
their members will want this product.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Animations for storytelling or decoration
- Large whitespace sections with no visual content
- Generic supplement brand photography — clean, bright, wellness-adjacent
- SaaS or dashboard-style layout patterns
- Typography as decoration — every word must earn its place
- Countdown timers, urgency tactics, or aggressive sales language
- Any layout that could belong to a different brand
- Centred text blocks on dark backgrounds as a default layout
- Features or sections not in the confirmed page structure

---

## Success Criteria

- A visitor understands the brand identity in under three seconds
- A gym owner or distributor feels compelled to make contact
- The site feels built, not templated
- Every section could only belong to Probell Nutrition
